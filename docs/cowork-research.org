This document is the *plan* for a new 50-iteration research loop
that extends the transfer-gradient research (=docs/transfer-gradient.org=)
from the reader-side dual-audience question to the author-side
human+agent cowork question.

*Status*: 2026-05-21 — plan only. The five MVP rules (=rules/lp-cowork-*.md=)
were shipped first, grounded in 6+ months of observed practice. The
loop's purpose is to *validate and deepen* the MVP, not to bootstrap
from scratch.

* Why this loop now

Symmetry with the reader-side research:

| Reader-side (done) | Author-side (this loop) |
|--------------------+--------------------------|
| Seed: NN/g 1997-2017 reader research transfers to AI agents | Seed: 6+ months of <reference-project> + edo + cmux cowork practice produces stable patterns |
| 10 HI directions (foraging, CLT, ...) | 10 CSCW / co-authoring / collaboration directions |
| 5 rounds × 6 questions per direction | Same structure |
| Output: figure + families + 8 observations + 6 doctrine rules | Output: figure + families + observations + N new doctrine rules |
| Question: which HI findings transfer to AI agent readers? | Question: which CSCW + co-author findings transfer to human-agent author pairs? |

The MVP rules were derived from *practice*. The loop derives them
from *research*. The two paths cross-validate.

Each direction answers the same 6 questions as the reader-side
research:

1. Specific claim (named principle + canonical wording).
2. Empirical basis (study, N, method, year).
3. Does the agent-as-author show isomorphic behaviour?
4. If yes — shared primitive?
5. If no — transfer break? (substrate / asymmetric capability / stake / tacit knowledge?)
6. Operational takeaway for the LP cowork author.

* Five rounds (same shape as transfer-gradient)

1. *Round 1 (iters 1-10)*: skeleton — one section per direction, 5 sub-headers each.
2. *Round 2 (iters 11-20)*: deepen — canon excerpts, empirical findings, first transfer assessment.
3. *Round 3 (iters 21-30)*: synthesise — cowork-gradient figure, shared-primitive families, cross-cutting observations.
4. *Round 4 (iters 31-40)*: cite + verify — WebSearch/WebFetch every non-trivial claim; populate Sources.
5. *Round 5 (iters 41-50)*: polish + trim; remove working-log scaffolding.

* Open questions before launching the loop

These are the points I want to confirm with the human before
running 50 iters. Pre-loop alignment prevents wasted iterations.

** Q1: scope — author-author OR collaboration-broader

The reader-side research stayed narrowly in HI / web-reader
territory. The cowork-side question can be framed two ways:

- *Narrow*: HCI / CSCW research on *human-human* co-authoring,
  asking which findings transfer to *human-agent* pairs. Tight
  analog to the reader-side question. Directions A, B, D, F, G are
  in this scope.
- *Broad*: also include LLM-specific findings (sycophancy
  measurement, calibration, etc.) where there is no human-human
  precedent but the failure mode is real. Directions C, E expand
  the scope.

The proposed table above mixes both. Need user input on whether to
narrow.

** Q2: validation — observational OR experimental

Reader-side research was 100% *observational* (citation review +
agent-trace inspection). Cowork-side could:

- Stay observational (same level of evidence as reader-side; cheaper).
- Add controlled experiments (e.g. measure sycophancy rate before
  vs after =lp-cowork-anti-sycophancy.md= adoption; needs a corpus
  + scoring).

Experimental data would lift the rigour but cost time.

** Q3: rules-as-input — MVP fixed OR revisable

The MVP rules (=lp-cowork-stake-declaration=, =-propose-before-edit=,
=-anti-sycophancy=, =-handoff-template=, =-persistence-stack=) were
shipped from practice before the research. Two stances:

- *MVP fixed*: research deepens and adds *new* rules; MVP rules
  stay as-is (with optional "Why this works" grounding sections
  added per Phase 2 pattern).
- *MVP revisable*: research can rewrite or merge MVP rules if it
  finds the practice was off.

Reader-side precedent: the 4 existing rules from <reference-project> got
"Why this works" grounding sections added but were not rewritten.
Defaulting to MVP-fixed unless user prefers otherwise.

** Q4: deliverable shape — one .org OR multi-file

Reader-side delivered one large .org (3243 lines). Could deliver
cowork-side the same way, or split into multiple smaller files
(one per direction, with =docs/cowork-research/= subdir).

** Q5: loop runtime — synchronous OR cron-paced

Reader-side ran *synchronously* (50 turns in one extended session).
Could also use the =/loop= or cron mechanism for ~1 iter every few
hours over days.

Cron-paced has the advantage of natural break + re-grounding
between iters; synchronous has the advantage of cache continuity.
