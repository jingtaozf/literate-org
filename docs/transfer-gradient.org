#+TITLE: Transfer Gradient — Which HI Findings Port to LP-with-AI
#+SUBTITLE: Research synthesis behind literate-agent's LP doctrine
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil num:nil

Synthesised summary of a 50-iteration research loop that asked
*which known human-interface (HI) commonalities transfer to literate
programming with AI agents, with what completeness, and where does
the transfer break?* The loop covered ten HI directions (information
foraging, cognitive load theory, working memory limits, eye-tracking,
mental models + dual-process, recognition vs recall, progressive
disclosure, Gestalt principles, berry-picking, affordances +
signifiers). Each direction was tested against the same six questions
(claim → empirical basis → agent-side isomorphism → shared primitive
→ where transfer breaks → operational takeaway).

This document is the *reference doc* the literate-agent rules cite.
It is NOT meant to be read top-to-bottom on every visit; LP authors
arriving at a specific rule (e.g. =rules/lp-load-bearing-affordances-
structural.md=) can follow the back-reference into the figure or
the relevant family table.

* The transfer-gradient figure
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: figure
:END:

Two axes:

- *Vertical* — substrate distance. Top = pure behaviour / decision
  structure. Bottom = motor or perceptual mechanism.
- *Horizontal* — transfer completeness for AI-agent reader. Left =
  full transfer. Right = transfer breaks at some identified boundary.

Three directions (A, F, I) cluster in the *upper-left full-transfer
quadrant*. Two directions (D, H) cluster in the *lower-right
human-only quadrant*. The remaining five spread along the partial-
transfer diagonal, each breaking at a different mechanism layer.

* Six shared-primitive families
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: families
:END:

The directions that transfer (or partially transfer) share small
families of primitives. A LP rule citing a specific primitive should
name the family so the family's transfer profile is visible.

| Family | Members | Primitive |
|--------+---------+-----------|
| Foraging-economics | A, F, I | Bounded budget + unequal landscape + asymmetric proximal-distal cost; closed-form math (Charnov 1976 marginal-value theorem). |
| Element-interactivity bound | B, C | Items × pairwise interactivity hits a finite store; structural form transfers, numerical constant does not. |
| Cross-session state persistence | B, E, G, I | Finding requires reader to carry state from one encounter to the next; break boundary at the agent's stateless cold-start. |
| Substrate-bound numerical constants | C, D, H | A specific number (Miller 7±2, Cowan 4±1, Rayner saccade lengths, Wertheimer grouping radii) is part of the canonical finding and derives from biological apparatus; the structural form survives but the number does not. |
| Perceptual-substrate-only | D, H | Entire finding lives in human visual-perception apparatus; stripping the perceptual mechanism leaves nothing portable. |
| Dual-process processing modes | E, F, G | Readers shift between fast cue-driven and slow deliberate processing depending on stake; agent traces show structural-form analogue (grep-scan vs chain-of-thought). |

*F and E are cross-family hubs* — they each appear in two families.
F's recognition asymmetry is the mechanism that makes foraging and
dual-process design work; E's dual-process frame names F and G's
joint structural role.

* Eight cross-cutting observations
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: observations
:END:

1. *Cross-session state persistence is a universal transfer-break
   boundary* (B, E, G, I). Codified by =rules/lp-agent-persistence-hooks.md=.
2. *Convergent design without lineage* — Anthropic skill SDK
   =when_to_use= ≈ Nielsen 1995 first-screen scannability;
   =:CUSTOM_ID:= ≈ Norman 2013 signifier; =allowed-tools:= ≈
   Tognazzini progressive-disclosure scaffolding.
3. *Substrate-level numerical limits do not transfer* — Miller 7±2,
   Cowan 4±1, Rayner saccade lengths, Wertheimer grouping radii are
   all substrate-bound constants. The structural form (limits exist)
   is universal; the numerical value is human-only.
4. *Behaviour and decision-structure findings transfer cleanly* —
   A, F, I anchor the upper-left quadrant because they describe
   behaviour shape, not substrate mechanism.
5. *Cross-family hubs* (F, E) — directions co-locate by shared
   underlying constraint structure, not topical proximity.
6. *Component-level rather than direction-level transfer* — some
   directions braid two findings with different transfer profiles
   (B braids CLT-as-load and CLT-as-schema-construction; E braids
   Norman's mental-model with Kahneman's dual-process).
7. *Convergent design across substrates validates the behaviour-vs-
   substrate axis* at meta-level — the convergent designs all live
   in the behaviour layer, not the substrate.
8. *Weak metaphors invite overgeneralisation* — surface analogies
   that lack closed-form math OR behavioural-isomorphism traces
   must NOT be codified as rules. Codified by
   =rules/lp-transfer-discipline-no-weak-metaphors.md=.

* Operational takeaways (per direction)
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: takeaways
:END:

Each direction's *Operational takeaway* compressed to one sentence
+ the literate-agent rule(s) where it landed.

| Dir | Takeaway | Lands in |
|-----+----------+----------|
| A. info foraging | Strengthen proximal cues (concept-named headings); reduce misleading scent; minimise patch-leave friction (cross-refs). | =literate-programming-document-first=, =lp-cross-file-link-form=, =lp-load-bearing-affordances-structural= |
| B. cognitive load | Reduce extraneous load (no redundant prose); one new interacting mechanism per section; cross-references and revisits over single-exposure schema. | =lp-prose-no-self-narration=, =lp-module-section-hierarchy=, =lp-agent-persistence-hooks= |
| C. working memory | Limit simultaneously-interacting concepts per section; use named chunks; do NOT chase the specific number (substrate-bound). | =lp-module-section-hierarchy=, =lp-transfer-discipline-no-weak-metaphors= |
| D. eye-tracking | Human-only optimisation: short paragraphs, front-loaded sentences, consistent typographic cues. Load-bearing affordances must NOT live only here. | =lp-load-bearing-affordances-structural= (warning) |
| E. mental models | Make designer's mental model explicit in prose; signal where deliberation is needed (numbered steps, named intermediate facts). | =literate-programming-document-first= (intent-before-implementation) |
| F. recognition/recall | LP preambles provide the cue; cross-references are pure recognition surfaces; failure mode is symmetric (hallucination vs "I knew that but couldn't find it"). | =lp-stable-anchors-for-multi-referenced-sections=, =lp-load-bearing-affordances-structural= |
| G. progressive disclosure | Multi-scale disclosure (CLAUDE.md → skills → bodies); name the trigger (`when_to_use`); decision-time disclosure transfers, skill-building disclosure does not. | Plugin =skills/= shape, =rules/literate-programming-document-first= |
| H. Gestalt | Typographic infrastructure serves humans only; structural infrastructure serves both audiences. Don't trade structural for typographic when they compete. | =lp-load-bearing-affordances-structural= |
| I. berry-picking | Design for path-dependent (grep-arrived) reading; support sufficient-not-optimal stopping; provide explicit persistence hooks for agent readers. | =lp-cross-file-link-form=, =lp-stable-anchors-for-multi-referenced-sections=, =lp-agent-persistence-hooks= |
| J. affordances + signifiers | 4-rule heuristic — dense in structural signifiers; structural > typographic for load-bearing; layer signifier vocabularies; heading-as-concept restated. | =lp-load-bearing-affordances-structural= (THIS direction is the rule's primary source) |

* When to consult this doc

- When adding a new LP rule: check against the figure (does it
  derive from a quadrant?) and the families (does it apply a shared
  primitive?), and verify against =rules/lp-transfer-discipline-no-
  weak-metaphors.md='s acceptance tests.
- When debating whether a typographic vs structural change is worth
  doing: check the family table for the affordance you're moving.
- When onboarding a new LP author: this doc is the *background*
  context for "why does literate-agent's doctrine look this way."

* Source

Synthesised from a 50-iteration research loop (May 2026, source
notebook gitignored in the original research project). Each iteration
produced one section per direction across five rounds (skeleton,
deepen, synthesise, cite + verify, polish + trim). All non-trivial
empirical claims were verified via primary-source citation in
Round 4.
